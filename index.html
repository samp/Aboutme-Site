About me

Hello, I'm Sam Palmer, a first year cybersecurity student at Staffordshire university. Computer science links strongly with my strength in Mathematics- I enjoy problem solving, starting on a problem and then working to find the solution. I enjoy the way aspects of computer science are logical and progressional, and find it interesting to expand upon patterns and sequences found in both mathematical concepts and computer processing.

I have completed

  • A-level mathematics
  • A-level computer science
  • A-level physics
  • 10 GCSEs

I have had an interest in technology since primary school; in ICT lessons, I was fascinated with the simple programming tools such as Scratch and Kodu that we used in class. Spurred on by the encouragements of my teachers, I then went on to study Computer Science at GCSE, further expanding my knowledge and sparking my curiosity and eventually leading to independently researching, planning, and building my own computer. Since then, I have upgraded both my own and a friend's computer.

Skills

Through my Computer Science GCSE, I learnt to program in Visual Basic and completed two projects, a mathematics-based population simulation and a simple graphical game, which laid the foundation for learning another programming language, Python, and undertaking another, more complex, project at A level- an app designed to provide day-to-day assistance with simple tasks including weather and reminder functions. Despite this, most of my knowledge is self-taught, from reading blogs, articles, and books in my free time.

During my Computer Science A-Level I have learnt to take a measured and logical approach to problems, which allows me to easily tackle new challenges. Although I have not personally studied Decision Maths in my Mathematics A-Level, many of my friends did. Seeing their use of algorithms and complex problem-solving inspired me to try to learn to solve the Rubik's cube - a task I am still working on to this day- demonstrating my initiative, perseverance and independence. My Physics A-Level has taught me to be curious about the world around me and has given me valuable research and experimental skills, allowing me to apply these skills in the real world and in my other subjects.

My skills include:

  1. Python programming
  2. Web development
  3. Mathematics
  4. Cisco IOS
